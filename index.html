The Problem

Alert Fatigue: Compliance teams get 2,000+ alerts/week. 95% are false positives.

Reactive vs Proactive: By the time specific rules trigger (e.g., "$5,000 threshold"), the money is already gone.

"Client deposits $500, trades for 10 minutes with $0.01 profit, then withdraws. That's laundering. But rules miss it."

The Challenge

How might we build an AI system that detects money laundering in real-time while turning 2,000 weekly alerts into 50 high-confidence cases?

  • Minimise false positives to prevent analyst burnout.
  • Explainable AI: Tell regulators *why* it's suspicious.
  • Unsupervised Learning: Find fraud types nobody has seen yet.
